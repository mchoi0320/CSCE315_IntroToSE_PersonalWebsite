About Me

I am a senior at Texas A&M University pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science with a focus in Statistics, and here is a little story.

Back when I was still a senior in high school, my dream major was Biomedical Engineering, so how did I end up in Computer Science instead? Ironically, the first spark for my interest in the field lit up during my freshman year of high school. I took a mandatory introductory computer science class, where I learned to use Scratch, Python, HTML, and Alice. After the year ended, however, I shifted my focus back to biomedical engineering in order to somewhat achieve my childhood dream of medically helping people.

Then came freshman year of college at Texas A&M. Once again, I had to take a mandatory introductory programming course. Once again, my interest in computer science sparked. But this time, it grew into a fire and there was no stopping it—I had way too much fun in the course! So here I am, still loving computer science as much as I did when I was still printing, “Hello, World!” despite the headaches it comes with nowadays.

I never hesitate to say that I am passionate about computer science. I may not be the best, but I certainly am beyond willing and am quick to learn new things such as languages, frameworks, etc. I am a flexible and positive individual who knows the importance of communication in teamwork.

In my leisure time, I like to play video games and watch the most random videos online ranging from gaming clips to cooking tutorials to videos of adorable animals. I absolutely love animals, hence the picture of me with Reveille! Recently, I have become interested in learning to play sports like basketball, volleyball, and tennis properly instead of just playing for fun. I also really love to sing until I get tired!

Portfolio

DePlane

DePlane is a web application product developed as a team during TAMUhack 2020. It allows the user to both obtain information about their landing destination and control features found during flight. We are proud to have created our own back-end service comprising of multiple APIs, as it made accessing our data much easier and faster. Also, we were able to implement the caching of different results from the APIs to limit the requests made to the services. Through this project, we learned how to implement Google's APIs and how to access APIs resourcefully to not go over any limits presented with the services.

Polynomial Applications Solver

The objective of this project was to write a C++ program for manipulating polynomials, including reading (parsing from strings), writing, finding sums and products, evaluation, taking derivatives, and calculating roots.

Elevator Simulator

The goal of this project was to write a simulation of an elevator system. I made a model of a building with f floors served by c elevator cars. It is an event-driven simulator, where, with each time-step, new passengers can arrive on floors, elevators can move between floors, and/or pick-up/drop-off passengers.

Resolution Proof Program

This project was written to perform logical proofs using Resolution Refutation. In addition, I created a complementary program that generates knowledge base files for the Wumpus World problem, which can be used as an input to the main program.

Services

Skeet Shoot Fundraising Event

The Hidalgo-Starr County A&M Club hosted their 8th Annual Skeet Shoot in October 2018 to raise money for the Club's scholarship funds, which are awarded to high school seniors who live in Hidalgo-Starr County that have been admitted to Texas A&M University in College Station, Galveston, or McAllen. As a volunteer, I helped set up for the event and assisted the participants throughout the competition.

The Big Event: McAllen

On Saturday, March 30, 2019, 82 Aggies, former students, and Aggie family members came together to participate in The Big Event: McAllen. The Big Event is the nation’s largest one-day service project where students show appreciation for their community’s continued support of Texas A&M University students during their college careers. The BIG Event: McAllen provided support to the Food Bank of the Rio Grande Valley where volunteers helped facilitate food access and safety for the 64,000+ individuals that the Food Bank of the Rio Grande Valley served during the first week of April in 2019. This impact includes the 1,200 food packages assembled by Aggies that were distributed to senior citizens in the community.

Food Bank of the Rio Grande Valley, Inc.

After my experience at The Big Event, I returned to the Food Bank of the Rio Grande Valley to volunteer because I genuinely felt the importance of helping at the facility to assist in packaging and distributing nutritious food quickly yet safely to those who need it. I also helped to clean and maintain a sanitary work area.
